  • The Types of Goods


    Complementary goods: What you need for something else to work. EX: Gasoline and Cars

    Substitute goods: It’s a substitute product with the same use as another.

    Demerit goods: Products that aren’t good for society, but are good for the economy. EX: cigarettes

    Merit goods: Products that are good for society and are good for the economy

    Normal goods: Items that people tend to buy more of when their incomes increase. Ex: New cars, TVs, clothes.

    Inferior goods: Items that people purchase more of when their incomes decrease. These are low cost options for saving money. Ex: Ramen Noodles, used clothes, canned bean and frozen dinners.

    Luxury goods: Items that people tend to buy when their incomes are extremely high. These items can be fancy cars, watches, clothes, etc.

    Necessities: Items that are needed to survive. Ex: water and food.
